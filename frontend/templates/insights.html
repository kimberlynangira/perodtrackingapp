Your Period Insights Through the Years

Explore how your menstrual cycle evolves from adolescence to menopause.

The Teenage Years (Menarche)

Menarche, the onset of menstruation, typically occurs between ages 10 and 16. During this time, cycles may be irregular as the body establishes hormonal balance.

  • Expect cycle length and flow variability.
  • Cramps and mood swings may begin and change.
  • Tracking helps understand changes and build healthy habits.

The Reproductive Years (20s-40s)

In your 20s and 30s, cycles tend to become more regular. This is the prime time for fertility, and hormonal fluctuations play a key role in the menstrual cycle.

  • Cycle length is usually more predictable.
  • PMS symptoms may become more pronounced.
  • Tracking is valuable for family planning.

Hormone Levels During Reproductive Years

[Reproductive Hormone Chart]

Perimenopause (40s-50s)

Perimenopause is the transition to menopause. Hormone levels fluctuate significantly, leading to changes in the menstrual cycle.

  • Cycles may become irregular.
  • Expect hot flashes and sleep disturbances.
  • Tracking helps in understanding changes.

Menstrual Cycle Changes in Perimenopause

[Perimenopause Cycle Chart]

Menopause and Beyond (50s+)

Menopause is marked by the cessation of menstruation. While periods stop, long-term health and well-being remain important.

  • Menstruation has ceased.
  • Focus on post-menopausal health.
  • Health tracking is still useful.

Basic Self-Care Tips for a Happy System

  • Eat a balanced diet: Focus on whole foods.
  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water.
  • Exercise regularly: Aim for moderate activity.
  • Get enough sleep: Prioritize rest.
  • Manage stress: Practice relaxation techniques.
  • Track your cycle: Understand your body.
  • Maintain hygiene: Keep your body clean.
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol.
  • Schedule regular check-ups: See your doctor.
  • Listen to your body: Pay attention to symptoms.
