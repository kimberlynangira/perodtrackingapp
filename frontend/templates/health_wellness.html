Health & Wellness for Women

Understanding Your Menstrual Cycle

Your menstrual cycle is a complex process that affects many aspects of your health. It's more than just your period! Understanding the different phases can help you better manage your physical and emotional well-being. The average cycle is 28 days, but it can vary.

Key phases include:

  • Menstruation (Days 1-5): This is when you have your period. Hormone levels are low.
  • Follicular Phase (Days 6-14): Your body prepares to release an egg. Estrogen rises.
  • Ovulation (Around Day 14): An egg is released. This is when you're most fertile.
  • Luteal Phase (Days 15-28): The body prepares for a possible pregnancy. Progesterone rises.

Tracking your cycle can help you predict your period, identify patterns, and understand changes in your body.

Healthy Habits for Overall Well-being

Here are some general health tips:

  • Balanced Diet: Eat a variety of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein.
  • Regular Exercise: Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise most days of the week.
  • Adequate Sleep: Get 7-8 hours of quality sleep each night.
  • Stress Management: Practice relaxation techniques like yoga, meditation, or deep breathing.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day.

Tips for Menstrual Health

Here are some tips for supporting your menstrual health:

  • Track Your Cycle: Use a calendar or app to monitor your periods, symptoms, and flow.
  • Manage Pain: Over-the-counter pain relievers, heat packs, and gentle exercise can help with cramps.
  • Eat Iron-Rich Foods: Include foods like red meat, beans, and leafy greens to replace iron lost during your period.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drinking water can reduce bloating and other symptoms.
  • Listen to Your Body: Rest when you need to, and adjust your activities based on how you feel.

When to Seek Medical Advice

It's important to consult a healthcare professional if you experience any of the following:

  • Severe pelvic pain
  • Heavy or prolonged bleeding
  • Irregular or missed periods
  • Unusual vaginal discharge
  • Concerns about your menstrual cycle or reproductive health
